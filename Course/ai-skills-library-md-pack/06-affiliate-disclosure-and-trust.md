# 06 — Affiliate Disclosure and Trust Guidance

## Principle

The AI Skills Library should be trust-first.

Affiliate potential should never be the main reason a resource is included.

Free, useful and credible resources should be prioritised.

## Suggested site disclosure

Use this in the AI Skills Library section:

> Some links may be affiliate links. If you use them, we may earn a small commission at no extra cost to you. We only include resources that appear genuinely useful, and free resources are prioritised wherever possible.

## Suggested trust statement

> This library prioritises free and low-cost resources from credible providers. Paid courses are only included where they appear genuinely useful for a specific role, skill or learning outcome. Any affiliate links will be clearly marked.

## Resource-level labels

Each resource should be labelled clearly.

Suggested labels:

- Free
- Free audit
- Paid certificate
- Paid
- Affiliate link
- No affiliate relationship
- Needs verification

## Affiliate research rules

When researching affiliate opportunities:

- Use official provider pages where possible.
- Use recognised affiliate networks where official pages are not available.
- Do not rely on random blog posts for commission data.
- Do not guess commission rates.
- Separate paid-course availability from affiliate-programme availability.
- Clearly mark unclear cases.

## Recommended order of priority

1. Include the best free resources first.
2. Verify whether paid certificate platforms have affiliate programmes.
3. Apply only to affiliate programmes that fit the trust-first positioning.
4. Clearly mark affiliate links on the site.
5. Never make affiliate income invisible.

## Warning

Perplexity may overstate affiliate availability unless asked to use official provider or recognised affiliate network sources only.

Always manually verify before publishing affiliate claims.
