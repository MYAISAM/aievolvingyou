# AI Evolving You — AI Skills Library Research & Ingestion Pack

This folder preserves the working context from the ChatGPT thread focused on building an AI upskilling section inside the AI Evolving You Resources area.

The purpose is to support account migration and provide reusable project documentation for GitHub.

## Main aim of the thread

Build out a new **AI Skills Library** within the wider **Resources** section of AI Evolving You.

The section should curate free and low-cost AI courses, guides, learning pathways and reports for people who want to upskill in AI, especially in relation to role change, AI displacement risk, and future-of-work anxiety.

The library should not be a generic link dump. It should be structured around real users, roles and use cases.

## Strategic role in the wider site

The Resources section is expected to have several lanes:

1. **Candidate / individual support**
   - Interview guides
   - CV and application support
   - Confidence and value articulation
   - Leads into the Interview Coach tool

2. **Organisation resources**
   - Free articles and opinion pieces
   - AI bias checklist
   - Procurement questionnaire
   - Hiring fairness and governance digital downloads

3. **AI upskilling resources**
   - Free and low-cost AI courses
   - Role-based learning pathways
   - AI literacy and productivity resources
   - Responsible AI and ethics learning

4. **AI displacement and labour-market intelligence**
   - Reports from Anthropic, Gartner, Stanford, WEF, OECD, IMF, etc.
   - New role predictions
   - AI exposure and task displacement studies
   - Leads into Evolve

## Recommended section name

Preferred direction:

> **AI Skills Library**

Suggested description:

> Free and low-cost courses, guides and learning pathways to help people understand, use and adapt to AI at work.

Alternative names considered:

- AI Upskilling Directory
- Learn AI by Role
- AI Skills Library

## Core principle

Start with the user’s role or concern, not with AI hype.

Suggested positioning line:

> Start with your role, not the hype.
